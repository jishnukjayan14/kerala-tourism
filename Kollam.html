INTRODUCTION

Kollam, situated in the southern Indian state of Kerala, is a captivating district renowned for its rich cultural heritage and scenic beauty, making it a gem in the realm of tourism. Nestled along the tranquil shores of the Arabian Sea, Kollam boasts an extensive coastline adorned with pristine beaches, including the popular Thirumullavaram Beach and the serene Tangasseri Beach. The district is a haven for history enthusiasts, with its ancient ports and historical landmarks such as the Thangassery Lighthouse, which stands as a testament to its maritime significance. Kollam is also famous for its unique backwater experiences, offering enchanting houseboat cruises along the Ashtamudi Lake, one of the largest and most picturesque backwaters in Kerala. The district is a cultural melting pot, featuring vibrant festivals like the Kollam Pooram and the traditional art forms of Kathakali and Mohiniyattam. With its blend of natural wonders and cultural treasures, Kollam beckons travelers seeking an authentic and immersive experience in the heart of Kerala's tourism landscape.
