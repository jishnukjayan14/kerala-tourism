INTRODUCTION

Kozhikode, located on the southwest coast of India in the state of Kerala, is a captivating district renowned for its rich historical heritage, scenic beauty, and cultural vibrancy, making it a prominent destination in the realm of tourism. Fondly known as the "City of Spices" and historically significant for being the place where Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama landed in 1498, Kozhikode seamlessly blends the old-world charm with modern allure. The district boasts pristine beaches like Kappad, where history and tranquility converge. The architectural marvels of the region, such as the ancient temples, mosques, and forts, narrate tales of a bygone era. Additionally, Kozhikode's delectable cuisine, influenced by Arab, Portuguese, and Dutch flavors, beckons food enthusiasts to savor its unique culinary offerings. With a perfect mix of history, nature, and cultural diversity, Kozhikode stands as an enticing destination for those seeking an immersive and memorable tourism experience.
